\begin{abstract}
Image Aesthetic Assessment (IAA) has attracted wide interest in recent years, but it remains challenging due to its highly subjective and ambiguous nature. With the prevalence of social media, user comments can provide rich aesthetics-aware semantic information and human observers typically evaluate images from the perspective of aesthetic attributes. Recently, learnable queries in large-scale pre-trained models have been shown to effectively extract task-specific features from frozen encoders. Motivated by this, we propose Learnable Query-Guided Visual-Comment Interaction Network (LQ-VCINet) for IAA, which includes Learnable Queries Guided Intra-Cross modal Interaction module (LQ-ICI) to perform hierarchical feature fusion, a global token guided multiple layer feature aggregation module (GFA), and an attribute guided representation refinement module (ARR) and a prediction head to perform aesthetic assessment. Experiments on AVA-Comments dataset demonstrate that LQ-VCINet achieves state-of-the-art performance on multimodal IAA, with an SRCC of 0.915 and a PLCC of 0.920.
\end{abstract}
\begin{IEEEkeywords}
Image aesthetic assessment, learnable queries, multimodal fusion, hierarchical interaction, aesthetic attributes.
\end{IEEEkeywords}

\section{Introduction}

\IEEEPARstart{I}{mage} aesthetic assessment (IAA) aims to automatically predict human aesthetic perception of images~\cite{datta2006,deng2017survey,anwar2022comparative}. Owing to its wide applications in fields such as image retrieval, photo recommendation, automatic enhancement, and social media content management, this task has attracted growing interest in recent years~\cite{murray2012ava,talebi2018nima,ren2017personalized}.

Due to its highly abstract nature, IAA remains a challenging research topic. Early IAA approaches focused on designing hand-crafted features based on photographic rules~\cite{marchesotti2011generic,dhar2011high}. More recently, with the prevalence of deep learning~\cite{mai2016composition,ma2017alamp,wang2016bdn}, distribution prediction methods (such as NIMA~\cite{talebi2018nima}) utilize Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) to predict aesthetic score distributions. However, relying solely on aesthetic score labels provides limited supervision information, which may result in a neglect of aesthetic details. Moreover, user comments can provide complementary aesthetics-aware semantic information~\cite{ke2023vila,xiong2024mmlq,chang2017pccd,ghosal2019aesthetic}, and structured aesthetic attributes offer explicit guidance for multimodal feature learning~\cite{kong2016parn,zhu2023aamnet,li2023ammnet,li2025critiques}, highlighting the value of jointly modeling visual, comment, and attribute information.

As illustrated in Fig.~\ref{fig:method_comparison}, existing IAA paradigms can be categorized into visual-only methods~\cite{talebi2018nima,ke2021musiq,he2022tanet,hou2025tcdg,li2025iaaclip,behrad2025charm}, comment-based multimodal methods~\cite{ke2023vila,xiong2024mmlq,zhang2021mscan}, and attribute-guided multimodal methods~\cite{zhu2023aamnet,li2023ammnet}. While visual-only methods are constrained by the lack of semantic context, comment-based methods leverage user-generated text to enrich aesthetic understanding. More recently, attribute-guided methods further incorporate structured aesthetic attributes to bridge visual and comment representations.

Recently, large-scale pre-trained models have demonstrated strong representation capabilities~\cite{dosovitskiy2021vit,li2022blip,li2023blip2}, and learnable queries have been shown to be effective in extracting task-specific features from frozen backbones~\cite{xiong2023iaalq,xiong2024mmlq}. Therefore, we propose LQ-VCINet for IAA. As shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:method_comparison}(c), unlike existing multimodal methods (Fig.~\ref{fig:method_comparison}(b)) that rely on single-level cross-modal fusion, LQ-VCINet performs hierarchical intra- and cross-modal interactions across multiple encoder layers with attribute-guided refinement, while keeping only lightweight fusion modules trainable~\cite{zhou2022coop,jia2022vpt,hu2022lora}.

The primary contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:
\begin{itemize}
\item We propose LQ-VCINet, a parameter-efficient multimodal framework for aesthetic score distribution prediction that integrates visual, comment, and attribute information by training only lightweight fusion modules on top of frozen pre-trained encoders.
\item We propose the LQ-ICI module, which applies learnable query-guided intra-cross interaction hierarchically. At each layer, queries first capture intra-modal aesthetic features and then exchange complementary information cross-modally.
\item We design GFA for aggregating hierarchical multi-level features via a global token, and ARR for refining the multimodal representation with aesthetic attribute priors, both contributing to improved aesthetic prediction.
\end{itemize}

\section{Method}

\subsection{Problem Formulation}

Given the $i$-th image and its corresponding user comment $(I_i,C_i)$, this paper aims to learn a mapping function $f(\cdot)$, which is formalized as:
\begin{equation}
\hat{\mathbf{y}}_i=f(I_i,C_i),
\end{equation}
where $\hat{\mathbf{y}}_i\in\mathbb{R}^{K}$ denotes the predicted aesthetic score distribution, $f(\cdot)$ represents the proposed assessment model, and $K$ is the number of score levels (e.g., $K=10$ for the AVA dataset). The predicted distribution satisfies the normalization constraint $\sum_{k=1}^{K}\hat{y}_i^k=1$. Based on the predicted distribution, the mean aesthetic score of the image can be further computed as:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:mean_score}
\mu_i=\sum_{k=1}^{K}k\cdot\hat{y}_i^k.
\end{equation}

Although images and their associated comments can provide rich aesthetic information, jointly modeling these two modalities remains challenging due to their modality gap, the multi-level nature of aesthetic perception, and the absence of structured semantic guidance for fusion.

To address these challenges, we propose LQ-VCINet (Fig.~\ref{fig:lqicif_overview}), which consists of three key modules: (1) learnable query-guided intra-cross interaction (LQ-ICI), (2) global token guided multiple layer feature aggregation module (GFA), and (3) attribute guided representation refinement module (ARR).

\subsection{Learnable query-guided intra-cross interaction}

Instead of directly applying cross-attention across all tokens, we employ learnable queries~\cite{xiong2023iaalq,xiong2024mmlq} to first extract intra-modal aesthetic features before performing cross-modal interactions. The scaled dot-product attention~\cite{vaswani2017attention} is defined as:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{Q},\mathbf{K},\mathbf{V})=
\mathrm{Softmax}\left(\frac{\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{K}^{\top}}{\sqrt{d}}\right)\mathbf{V},
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{Q}$, $\mathbf{K}$, and $\mathbf{V}$ denote the query, key, and value matrices respectively, and $d$ is the feature dimension. For brevity, $\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{Q},\mathbf{X})=\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{Q},\mathbf{X},\mathbf{X})$ when keys and values share the same source.

Self-attention is defined as a special case where queries, keys, and values all come from the same input:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{SA}(\mathbf{X})=\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{X},\mathbf{X},\mathbf{X}).
\end{equation}
When applying $D$ stacked self-attention layers, we denote it as $\mathrm{SA}^{(D)}(\cdot)$.

For the feature pair $(\mathbf{V}_l,\mathbf{T}_l)$ at layer $l$, where $\mathbf{V}_l$ and $\mathbf{T}_l$ denote the visual and textual features extracted from the $l$-th layer of CLIP image encoder~\cite{radford2021clip} and BERT~\cite{devlin2019bert} respectively, learnable queries $\mathbf{Q}_l^V,\mathbf{Q}_l^T\in\mathbb{R}^{N_q\times d}$ (where $N_q$ is the number of queries) first aggregate intra-modal aesthetic features via cross-attention:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
{\mathbf{R}}_l^V&=\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{Q}_l^V,\mathbf{V}_l),&
{\mathbf{R}}_l^T&=\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{Q}_l^T,\mathbf{T}_l).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Cross-modal attention then models the complementary relationship between visual and comment features:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\hat{\mathbf{R}}_l^V&=\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{R}_l^V,\mathbf{T}_l),&
\hat{\mathbf{R}}_l^T&=\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{R}_l^T,\mathbf{V}_l).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The intra- and cross-modal features are aggregated via mean fusion to obtain the layer-$l$ output representations:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{F}_l^V=\frac{{\mathbf{R}}_l^V+\hat{\mathbf{R}}_l^V}{2},\quad
\mathbf{F}_l^T=\frac{{\mathbf{R}}_l^T+\hat{\mathbf{R}}_l^T}{2}.
\end{equation}
Applying this process across all $L$ layers yields $\{\mathbf{F}_l^V\}_{l=1}^{L}$ and $\{\mathbf{F}_l^T\}_{l=1}^{L}$, progressively capturing hierarchical visual-comment interaction fusion information.

\subsection{ Global token guided multiple layer feature aggregation}

To capture multi-level aesthetic characteristics, we average each layer's output across the query dimension:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{Z}_l^V=\mathrm{Mean}(\mathbf{F}_l^V),\quad
\mathbf{Z}_l^T=\mathrm{Mean}(\mathbf{F}_l^T),\quad l=1,\ldots,L,
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{Z}_l^V,\mathbf{Z}_l^T\in\mathbb{R}^{d}$. The layer-wise features are then concatenated with learnable global tokens $\mathbf{g}^V,\mathbf{g}^T\in\mathbb{R}^{d}$, and processed by a $D$-layer stacked self-attention module after adding positional encodings:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{S}^{V}&=\mathrm{SA}^{(D)}([\mathbf{g}^{V};\mathbf{Z}_{1}^{V};\ldots;\mathbf{Z}_{L}^{V}]),\\
\mathbf{S}^{T}&=\mathrm{SA}^{(D)}([\mathbf{g}^{T};\mathbf{Z}_{1}^{T};\ldots;\mathbf{Z}_{L}^{T}]),
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
where $D=2$ and $\mathbf{S}^{V},\mathbf{S}^{T}\in\mathbb{R}^{(L+1)\times d}$. The first position of each output sequence (i.e., the global token position) is extracted as the holistic visual and textual representation:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{h}^{V}=\mathbf{S}^{V}[0],\quad \mathbf{h}^{T}=\mathbf{S}^{T}[0].
\end{equation}
Finally, the two modality representations are concatenated to form the multimodal fused feature:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{F}_{\mathrm{cat}}=[\mathbf{h}^{V};\mathbf{h}^{T}]\in\mathbb{R}^{2d}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Attribute-Guided Representation Refinement}

For fused $\mathbf{F}_{\mathrm{cat}}$, aesthetic attributes are further introduced to obtain attribute-aware features~\cite{kong2016parn,dhar2011high,celona2022composition}. The attribute encoder extracts a global attribute feature $\mathbf{g}\in\mathbb{R}^{M}$ and $A$ attribute scores $s^1,\ldots,s^A$ (we use 11 aesthetic attributes, thus $A=11$). Each attribute score is concatenated with $\mathbf{g}$ and projected into an attribute token via an attribute projection layer (linear + layer normalization + ReLU), forming the attribute token sequence:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{tok}}=
[\mathrm{Proj}([\mathbf{g};s^1]);\ldots;\mathrm{Proj}([\mathbf{g};s^A])]
\in\mathbb{R}^{A\times d}.
\end{equation}
Using $\mathbf{F}_{\mathrm{cat}}$ as the query and $\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{tok}}$ as keys and values, the fused feature is then calibrated via cross-attention (as defined in Eq.~(3)):
\begin{equation}
\hat{\mathbf{Y}}=\mathrm{Attn}(\mathbf{F}_{\mathrm{cat}},\mathbf{A}_{\mathrm{tok}})\in\mathbb{R}^{2d}.
\end{equation}
The refined feature $\hat{\mathbf{Y}}$ is subsequently fed into the prediction head for aesthetic score distribution prediction.

\subsection{Aesthetic Score Distribution Prediction}

The refined representation $\hat{\mathbf{Y}}_i$ is passed through an MLP head with softmax to produce the predicted score distribution:
\begin{equation}
\hat{\mathbf{y}}_i=\mathrm{Softmax}(\mathrm{MLP}(\hat{\mathbf{Y}}_i)).
\end{equation}
The normalized ground-truth $\mathbf{y}_i$ supervises $\hat{\mathbf{y}}_i$ via Earth Mover's Distance (EMD):
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{emd}} =
\left(
\frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^{K}
\left|
\mathrm{CDF}_{\hat{\mathbf{y}}_i}(k)-
\mathrm{CDF}_{\mathbf{y}_i}(k)
\right|^r
\right)^{1/r},
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{CDF}_{\hat{\mathbf{y}}_i}(k)=\sum_{j=1}^{k}\hat{y}_i^j$, $\mathrm{CDF}_{\mathbf{y}_i}(k)=\sum_{j=1}^{k}y_i^j$, and $r=2$. The final mean score is computed by \eqref{eq:mean_score}.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Experimental Protocols}

We evaluate our model on the AVA-Comments dataset, which provides 3,602,206 comments for 255,530 images~\cite{murray2012ava}. Following the standard evaluation protocol~\cite{talebi2018nima,hou2025tcdg}, we split the dataset into 222,383 images for training, 11,707 images for validation, and 19,805 images for testing. We report SRCC, PLCC, ACC, and EMD as evaluation metrics. The AVA score distribution is directly normalized as ground truth. All experiments are implemented in PyTorch on a single NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5060 Ti GPU.

\subsection{Implementation Details}

All comments matched to an image are concatenated as text input, while missing comments are replaced by a ``no comment'' token. Images are resized to $224\times224$ with random horizontal flipping during training. We freeze CLIP ViT-L/14 image encoder~\cite{radford2021clip}, BERT-base~\cite{devlin2019bert}, and the attribute encoder, and train only the Adapter modules, projection layers, and attention modules for 10 epochs using AdamW with learning rate $1\times10^{-4}$ and batch size 16.

\subsection{Performance Comparison}

We compare LQ-VCINet with representative IAA methods, including vision-only methods, comment-based multimodal methods, and attribute-guided multimodal methods, on the AVA-Comments dataset. The results are summarized in Table~\ref{tab:main_comparison}, from which several observations can be drawn:
\begin{itemize}
\item All visual-only methods show limited prediction accuracy, reflecting their inability to capture aesthetics-aware semantic information without textual context.
\item Comment-based multimodal methods outperform all visual-only approaches by incorporating user-generated comments, yet still exhibit limitations. For instance, AesMamba-M~\cite{gao2024aesmamba} surpasses the best visual-only method TCD-G~\cite{hou2025tcdg}, but still falls below our LQ-VCINet, suggesting that comment-based fusion alone is insufficient for capturing the full complexity of aesthetic perception.
\item Attribute-guided multimodal methods further improve performance by introducing aesthetic attributes to guide representation learning. Among these, our LQ-VCINet outperforms the previous $I+C+A$ state-of-the-art AMM-Net~\cite{li2023ammnet} across all metrics, demonstrating the effectiveness of learnable query-guided hierarchical interaction and attribute-guided refinement for multimodal aesthetic assessment.
\end{itemize}


\subsection{Ablation Study}

Table~\ref{tab:ablation} reports ablation results from single-modality baselines to the full LQ-VCINet model. The single-modality baselines ($A_0$ and $A_1$) show that neither visual nor comment features alone are sufficient for accurate aesthetic assessment, confirming the necessity of multimodal modeling. Simple concatenation ($A_2$) improves performance by confirming the complementarity of the two modalities, while direct cross-attention ($A_3$) brings further gains through explicit cross-modal interaction. Replacing direct cross-attention with our hierarchical intra- and cross-modal interaction ($A_4$) yields consistent improvements across all metrics, demonstrating the effectiveness of LQ-ICI in capturing richer multimodal representations. Finally, incorporating the attribute-guided representation refinement module ($A_5$) further improves performance, validating the benefit of aesthetic attribute priors for aesthetic quality assessment.

\subsection{Model Complexity Analysis}

Table~\ref{tab:complexity} compares LQ-VCINet with representative baselines in terms of trainable parameters (M), adaptation GMACs, and prediction performance (SRCC on AVA-Comments). LQ-VCINet achieves the highest SRCC of 0.915 with 175.870\,M trainable parameters and 10.837\,GMACs. Compared to the strongest multimodal baseline AesMamba-M (SRCC 0.892), our method delivers superior performance with more parameters but significantly higher accuracy. Notably, AMM-Net requires 420.408\,M parameters yet attains only SRCC 0.816, demonstrating that parameter scale alone does not guarantee better aesthetic assessment. These results confirm that LQ-VCINet achieves a favorable trade-off between prediction accuracy and model complexity. GMACs are measured with \texttt{thop} on a $224\times224$ image; for frozen-encoder methods, backbone computations are excluded.

\section{Conclusion}

In this letter, we proposed LQ-VCINet, a parameter-efficient multimodal framework for image aesthetic score distribution prediction. By combining learnable query-guided image-comment interaction, hierarchical aggregation, and attribute encoder-based refinement, the proposed method exploits complementary visual, comment, and aesthetic attribute information while keeping pretrained encoders frozen. Experiments on AVA-Comments demonstrate the effectiveness of combining user comments and aesthetic attribute priors for accurate image aesthetic assessment.
